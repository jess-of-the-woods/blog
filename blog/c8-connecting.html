Any other musings?

The three assumptions I thought were really cool. They are as follows:

  1. Assume that everybody in this room is here to serve the greater good, until proven otherwise.
  2. Given the above assumption, we therefore assume that none of us has any hidden agenda, until proven otherwise.
  3. Given the above assumption, we therefore assume that we are all reasonable even when we disagree, until proven otherwise.

These seem to me like things which would be useful in a variety of contexts beyond just meetings as suggested in the book. And kind of made me realise that sometimes I am quite cynical and assume the worst about people and their intentions, until proven otherwise. And this is something that I would like to reduce.
